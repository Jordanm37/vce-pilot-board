# Hard-exemplar injection: results

Generated by `scripts/gate/hard_exemplar_exp.py` on the 57 hardest dev parts (the parts that at least 4 of the 7 empirical-sweep solver configurations get wrong). Three solver configurations, three prompt arms, two passes each.

## What the arms are

- **Arm A (control):** the configuration's champion system prompt only.
- **Arm B (topic-similar):** champion prompt plus two worked exemplars chosen for topic similarity to the target part (same subtopic first, then same module and answer type, then same module).
- **Arm C (hardness-similar):** champion prompt plus two worked exemplars drawn from parts the sweep missed at least twice (i.e. other genuinely hard parts), preferring the same module. These show how hard questions get solved.

An exemplar is the gold question text, its verified worked solution, and its solve script where one exists. No exemplar is the target part, shares its stem (the part grouping before the last '/'), or comes from the same exam paper - so none leaks the answer.

## How to read the numbers

- **Delivered %**: fraction of the 57 parts answered correctly (rule-based grade with an LLM-judge rescue for answers the rule grader cannot parse).
- **fixed / broke** (paired): comparing two arms on the same parts, *fixed* = parts the first arm got wrong and the second got right; *broke* = right then wrong. Only the parts where the two arms disagree carry statistical signal.
- **McNemar p**: exact two-sided p-value for whether fixed and broke are imbalanced (the paired test for a yes/no outcome measured twice on the same items). Below ~0.05 would indicate a real directional effect.
- **Detection limit**: with 57 parts and 2 passes this experiment can only certify large effects. A swing of a handful of parts will not reach significance; treat anything with p above 0.05 as directional, not proven.
- **Re-roll variance**: arm A alone (no exemplars, just a fresh attempt) recovered 22-25% of each configuration's original sweep misses, so re-roll noise is the same size as any arm effect measured here - a further reason nothing certifies.

Sample: 3 configurations x 57 parts x 3 arms x 2 passes.

**Caveat on the C-vs-B contrast:** on 18 of the 57 targets the topic-similar (B) and hardness-similar (C) selections share at least one exemplar (a hard part in the same subtopic qualifies for both). Where they overlap, the two arms are less distinct, so the C-vs-B comparison understates any true difference between the two selection strategies.
